Story Summary

College sophmore Conner O'donnell is reaching the end of his second year, and still lacks direction of what he wants to pursue. Being told that he had much time to figure out what he wants to do, he ended up waiting for answers to come to him, only to realize that his time in college is passing by. Join Conner on his task of going out and trying to find the answers of what he should do with his life, exploring the possibilities and opportunities around Wood Creek University!

Character List

Conner O'donnell

Conner O'donnell is a sophmore student of Wood Creek University. Being one to go with the flow, Conner waited for answers to come to him, in hopes that he would eventually get an idea of what he wants to do with his life. Unfortunately time has passed, and Conner is running out of time before he has to declare a major. Realizing that he can no longer wait around for answers, he seeks experiences around the campus, to give him clarity of what he should make of himself.

About the Author

I made this story with the scenario of being an undeclared major for the first two years of my college life. The future has a lot to think about, and can be very difficult for people to figure out. Coming out to Michigan State University, didn't seem far, but I was still leaving all of my friends and family behind. Making friends was difficult in large lecture halls, and those that I did meet didn't stay in contact much after the semester ended. One thing that I was always told ever since I left high school was "You have plenty of time to decide you major". Big mistake believing that! So in the meantime, I would take all of my required credits. By the time I got most of those done, I still had no clue of what studies I wanted to focus on. I went towards education in the beginning, and ended up realizing that I don't enjoy teaching all that much. Off a limb, I decided to study Media and Information, only to learn that I wouldn't be able to graduate in the four years that I wanted to, and due to the fact that I took my time taking education classes, I lost some experience that I could have gotten with more Media classes. Now, in my senior year, I'm taking a semester off to make a portfolio for myself, take some time to figure out what I want to do with my life, and hopefully find some work that will give me some field experience.
